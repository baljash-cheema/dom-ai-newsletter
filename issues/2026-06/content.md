Welcome to the inaugural issue of *AI in Medical Education* — one paper worth
your time, each month, from the DOM Education AI in MedEd Subcommittee.

!!! takeaways "This issue at a glance"
    - **Supervise AI like any other tool** — a learner using AI is a teaching moment, not a violation.
    - **Run the DEFT-AI debrief** — a 2-minute script to coach any AI interaction (inside).
    - **Verify, *then* trust** — an answer that "sounds right" isn't evidence that it is.

## Article of the Month

**Educational Strategies for Clinical Supervision of Artificial Intelligence
Use.** Abdulnour RE, Gin B, Boscardin CK. *N Engl J Med.* 2025;393(8):786–797.[^1]

The question is no longer *whether* trainees use AI — it's how we **supervise**
it. Models sound fluent and authoritative, which is exactly why offloading real
reasoning onto them is risky: the review names **deskilling** (losing a skill),
**never-skilling** (never building it), and **mis-skilling** (learning the wrong
thing when an AI error goes unchallenged).[^1]

<div class="statcard">
  <div class="stat-num">1 in 3+</div>
  <div class="stat-text">In one study the review cites, more than a third of advanced medical students failed to catch <strong>erroneous</strong> AI answers to clinical cases.</div>
</div>

The fix isn't *less* AI — it's **critical thinking at the moment of use**: treat
any output you can't fully verify as a cue to pause, and **verify *then* trust**,
in that order.[^1]

### The DEFT-AI debrief — your 2-minute tool[^1]

| Step | Ask the learner | Listen for |
|------|-----------------|------------|
| **D** Diagnose | "What tool did you use, and what did you type in?" | Did AI replace or support reasoning? |
| **E** Evidence | "How did you verify it? Is the tool safe here?" | Verification habits; limits |
| **F** Feedback | "How would you rate your own use of AI?" | Over- or under-reliance |
| **T** Teach | *One point — a reasoning gap or an AI-literacy tip.* | — |
| **AI** Recommend | *Set a plan for next time.* | AI to **inform**, not replace.[^1] |

!!! note "Editor's Perspective — from the subcommittee"
    The real contribution here is cultural: DEFT-AI reframes a learner's AI use as
    a teaching moment, not something to police. Two honest caveats — it is a
    *proposed* model shown with one fictional vignette, not yet tested in real
    teaching, and a full debrief for *every* AI interaction is unrealistic under
    clinic time pressure. **Our suggestion:** adopt the posture today — name the
    moment, ask how they verified it — and save the full script for higher-stakes
    cases.
